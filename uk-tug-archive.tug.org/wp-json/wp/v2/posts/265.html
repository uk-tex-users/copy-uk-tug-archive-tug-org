BachoTeX 2009 \u2014 Call for Papers<\/h2>\n

Dear TeXies, TeX friends and lovers of fine typography \u2014<\/p>\n

This is an invitation to BachoTeX 2009, the XVIIth Polish TeX Users Group Conference.<\/p>\n

As usual, it will be held at the traditional TeXies’ and GUST meeting place, Bachotek near Brodnica, in the north-east of Poland, from April 29 until May 3, 2009 inclusive.<\/p>\n

We are again trying to get a glimpse of the future, whence the conference title:<\/p>\n

TeX: at a turning point, or at the crossroads?<\/em><\/p>\n

The community is putting a lot of effort and thought into possible strategies for promoting and developing TeX and related products for the foreseeable future, including :<\/p>\n

    \n
  • TeX, and TeX-based engines<\/li>\n
  • enhanced graphics engines<\/li>\n
  • new or improved macro packages<\/li>\n
  • user interfaces<\/li>\n
  • new fonts<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Work progresses in many different directions, and thus there is clearly hope for an ever better future, even though the more pessimistic may wonder whether TeX represents an evolutionary dead-end.<\/p>\n

    In this context the feedback between users and developers of new tools and engines is immensely important \u2014 it might decide whether within a few years we will be looking back at today as a successful turning point or as a bad decision at the crossroads of TeX’s history.<\/p>\n

    Some say that they have never believed that conference themes\/mottos have any impact on submissions anyway. We, the Program Committee, think otherwise; please help us to prove our point! Although we do not restrict what your presentations should be about, we would be more than happy if your contribution will be user-centric and oriented towards the future of TeX with special emphasis on the needs, hopes and dangers.<\/p>\n

    Proposals (abstracts) should be e-mailed to the Program Committee, which listens at the address of papers-2009 at gust dot org dot pl. Bogus\u0142aw Jackowski is the appointed chairman. Also, please note the \u201cCall for TeX Pearls\u201d below.<\/p>\n

    Especially welcome are proposals for TeX-related tutorials or introductions. If you have suggestions for tutorials or workshops by others than yourself or about specific topics, please let us know.<\/p>\n

    The deadline for abstracts and other proposals is the March 8th. The deadline for final papers will soon be published at the conference web site:<\/p>\n

    http:\/\/www.gust.org.pl\/BachoTeX\/2009<\/a><\/p>\n

    Recovery from stress<\/h2>\n

    Exhausted TeXies will get a chance to recover their intellectual powers during nightly musical sessions, usually at a bonfire. Participate by bringing your own instruments (and voices)! Nature fans will have the opportunity to enjoy the unspoiled features of the Bachotek lake and surrounding woods :<\/p>\n

    http:\/\/www.gust.org.pl\/bachotex\/2008\/gallery\/andrzej-odyniec\/20080502_122214_DSC0116.jpg<\/a><\/p>\n

    The conference could also be a family event \u2014 the conference site is an enclosed area with a safe and attractive playing ground for children and parents alike :<\/p>\n

    http:\/\/www.gust.org.pl\/bachotex\/2008\/gallery\/andrzej-odyniec\/20080502_192247_DSC0150.jpg<\/a><\/p>\n

    http:\/\/www.gust.org.pl\/bachotex\/2008\/gallery\/andrzej-odyniec\/20080501_161111_DSC0065.jpg<\/a><\/p>\n

    Please make the most of the opportunity !<\/p>\n

    Call for TeX Pearls<\/h2>\n

    We are continuing the tradition of \u201cThe Pearls of TeX Programming\u201d. Here, briefly, is what is wanted:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • short TeX, MF or MP macro(s)<\/li>\n
    • results must be useful, and the solution not obvious<\/li>\n
    • easy to explain : 10 minutes at most<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      If you have something that fits the bill, please consider submitting a proposal. If you know of somebody’s work that does the same, please let us know, and we will contact that person. Other details and previously collected Pearls can be found at<\/p>\n

      http:\/\/www.gust.org.pl\/projects\/pearls\/<\/a><\/p>\n
