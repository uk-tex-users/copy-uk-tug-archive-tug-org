This dropped into the UK-TUG inbox earlier today<\/p>\n

Dear TeXies, programmers, typographers – TeX friends and lovers of fine typography,<\/p>\n

This is the call for papers and invitation for BachoTeX 2010, the XVIIIth Polish TeX users Group conference. As usual, it will be held at the traditional TeXies’ and GUST meeting place, Bachotek near Brodnica, in the north-east of Poland, from April 30 until May 4, 2010, inclusive. This time we are especially trying to get-together typographers with programmers and designers of typography systems:<\/p>\n

Typographers and programmers: mutual inspirations<\/h2>\n

Typography, the art of composing signs and graphics into pages and designing signs and graphics is nowadays inadvertently linked with using software. Programmers design and create systems which serve as tools for typographic projects.<\/p>\n

During this conference we want to get together two groups of people: programmers and designers of typographic systems with typographers and other users of such systems. Share your experiences. Tell others how you are doing it. Let’s together look into what programmers can offer to those who create works containing letters and other signs and how programmers can profit from the knowledge and experience of the users of such systems, designers, typographers or creators of web pages.<\/p>\n

The members of GUST, organizers of the conference, are especially sensitive to typesetting precision, solicitude and dependability, both in creating the tools and using them. We’d like the typographic and programming elegance and solicitude to be reflected in the conference presentations.<\/p>\n

Especially invited are presentations on:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • typographic projects ranging from business cards designs through web pages up to multi-volume books; we are interested is problems encountered, technologies employed, drawbacks of available software, unfulfilled dreams, successes or barriers and obstacles.<\/li>\n
  • designing and creating programs and programming systems aimed and typographic goals: both batch and interactive typesetting systems, client-server realizations, especially such for web sites.<\/li>\n
  • use of typography where programmability is vital: database extracts or combining multimedia and typography.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Of course we will gladly accept presentations relating the above with the TeX typesetting system but that is not a requirement for acceptance.<\/p>\n

    Let this meeting of typographers and programmers creating helper systems for typography become a step towards beautiful results!<\/p>\n

    Submissions, deadlines and addresses<\/h3>\n

    Proposals (abstracts) should be e-mailed to the Program Committee, which listens at the address of prog-2010 at gust dot org dot pl. Boguslaw Jackowski (b underscore jackowski at gust dot org dot pl) is the appointed chairman. Also, please note the \u2018Call for TeX Pearls\u2019 below.<\/p>\n

    The deadline for abstracts and other proposals is March 28th 2010. The deadline for final papers to appear in the conference materials is April 10th, 2010.<\/p>\n

    The conference web pages, including a registration form, are at the canonical address: http:\/\/gust.org.pl\/bachotex\/2010<\/a>.<\/p>\n

    Call for TeX Pearls<\/h3>\n

    We are continuing the tradition of \u2018The Pearls of TeX Programming\u2019. Here is, briefly, what is wanted:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • short TeX, MF or MP macro(s)<\/li>\n
    • results must be useful, and the solution not obvious<\/li>\n
    • easy to explain : 10 minutes at most<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      If you have something that fits the bill, please consider submitting a proposal. If you know of somebody’s work that does the same, please let us know, and we will contact that person. Other details and previously collected pearls can be found at http:\/\/www.gust.org.pl\/projects\/pearls\/<\/a>. The dedicated email address is: pearls at gust dot org dot pl.<\/p>\n

      Jerzy Ludwichowski
      \nGUST, President<\/p><\/blockquote>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
