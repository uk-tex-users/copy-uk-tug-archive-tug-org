The UK TeX Users\u2019 Group (UK-TUG) periodically runs training courses in using LaTeX. We are very pleased to announce a presentation of our beginners course, loosely entitled \u2018Using LaTeX to write a thesis\u2019. Content will include:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Editing and compiling LaTeX documents<\/li>\n
  • Logical structure<\/li>\n
  • Chapters, sections, and other document divisions<\/li>\n
  • Including graphics<\/li>\n
  • Creating tables<\/li>\n
  • Bibliographies<\/li>\n
  • Dealing with large documents<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    The course will be taking place on Friday August 26th in central London<\/a>, and will run from approximately 10 a.m. to around 5 pm. The course will be aimed at new LaTeX users, with an emphasis on hands-on experience. The course will take place on user’s own laptops: remember to bring one along on the day!<\/p>\n

    The course fee is \u00a310 for current UK-TUG members, and \u00a325 for non-members. The fee includes the course itself, refreshments on the day and a copy of the TeX Live 2010 DVD. For non-members, it also includes membership of UK-TUG for until the end of 2012.<\/p>\n

    Places are strictly limited by the size of the venue. To book a provisional place, please e-mail joseph.wright@morningstar2.co.uk<\/a> with your details. The course fee and (for non-members) a copy of the membership form<\/a> should then be sent to Joseph Wright, UK-TUG Secretary. Payment should be sent within two weeks of making a provisional booking, otherwise the space may be released. We will also hold a \u2018reserve\u2019 list of names if the course reaches capacity: experience suggests that the course will book up very rapidly.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
