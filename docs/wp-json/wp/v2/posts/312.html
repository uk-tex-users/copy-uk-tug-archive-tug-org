We have an opportunity to advance and improve the mathematical use of TeX in the UK.<\/p>\n

JISC supports education and research in the UK by promoting innovation in new technologies. They have recently announced funding for workshops to discuss issues related to e-content and digitisation. Their announcement<\/a> gives mathematical journals as an example.<\/p>\n

This is part of a larger program. For example, their Scholarly Communications Working Group<\/a> have commissioned and published a report Key Concerns within the Scholarly Communication Process<\/em>,<\/a> which is likely to interest many UK users of TeX.<\/p>\n

Up to \u00a38,000 is available for each workshop. The deadline for proposals (which must be from a Higher Education institution in the UK) is Friday 8th May. If you’d like to attend such a workshop, or think that your employer might like to submit a TeX related proposal, please contact the Committee<\/a> as soon as possible. Jonathan Fine, UK-TUG Chair, has suggested that he ccould ask his employer, The Open University, to submit a proposal related to mathematical content, but that he iswilling to defer to another institution.
